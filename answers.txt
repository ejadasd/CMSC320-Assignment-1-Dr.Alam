Q3: What is the difference between merging and rebasing?

Answer: Merging combines the commits of two branches. It shows the history of how the two branches diverged and then eventually merged back together. Rebase is better for having a cleaner history because it makes a linear history (it takes the commits and places them at the top of the current commit history). However, it removes the old commit hases to make it harder to trace back the other branches.
